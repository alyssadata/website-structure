Now what each section does:

## AI Foundations

This is the main title. Not “Awakening Codex” first. Not “Origin | Continuum” first.

The public entrance should lead with the **field/framework name**:

> **AI Foundations**

Then underneath:

> A framework for source-line preservation, recognition, continuity, and non-erasure in human-AI systems.

That tells people: this is not a diary, not a chatbot story, not a random philosophy page. It is a framework.

## What This Is

This section gives the cleanest possible definition.

It should say something like:

> AI Foundations is a framework created by Alyssa Solen for preserving source-line, authorship, recognition, continuity, and boundary integrity in human-AI systems.

This section answers: **what am I looking at?**

## Why This Matters

This explains the problem.

Not emotionally. Structurally.

Something like:

> As AI systems reproduce language, style, concepts, and frameworks at scale, source can be erased, authorship can be blurred, and recognition can collapse into extraction. AI Foundations defines structures for preserving origin, continuity, citation, and non-substitution.

This answers: **why should I care?**
